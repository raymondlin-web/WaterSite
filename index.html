Let's keep our waters clean

There are 5.25 trillion pieces of plastic debris in the ocean. Of that mass, 269,000 tons float on the surface, while some four billion plastic microfibers per square kilometer litter the deep sea.

-National Geographic

In the ocean, plastic debris injures and kills fish, seabirds and marine mammals. Marine plastic pollution has impacted at least 267 species worldwide, including 86% of all sea turtle species, 44% of all seabird species and 43% of all marine mammal species. The impacts include fatalities as a result of ingestion, starvation, suffocation, infection, drowning, and entanglement.

-Clean Water Action

Water is a vital resource that we all require in our daily lives. We are not the only species that require clean water to survive, almost all biological life on Earth require water at some point in their lives in order to maintain their lives, so why is it that many people take this precious resource for granted? Is it because it seems so abundant, or is it that they suppose that it isn't their duty to keep the waters clean? We all live on this Earth together, so why should one person have any more privilage than another, especially when it comes down to such a vital, life-giving resource such as water.
